Research Directions & Interests

I develop machine-learning methods for computational oncology, aiming to identify therapeutic targets and robustly translate multi-omics evidence into clinical insight. Methodologically, my work spans bias/selection-shift mitigation, semi-supervised & metric learning, graph-based modeling, and multi-omics integration for tasks such as synthetic-lethality prediction, patient stratification, and target prioritization.

  • Bias & distribution shift: selection-bias diagnosis/mitigation, fair evaluation under shift.
  • Semi-supervised & metric learning with diversity guidance; reliability under limited labels.
  • Graphs & embeddings: pathway/interaction-aware representations for biology.
  • Multi-omics integration: combining transcriptomics, genomics, and functional screens.

Projects

Metric-DST

Bias mitigation • Semi-supervised metric learning • Target discovery

Diversity-guided semi-supervised metric learning that reduces selection bias and improves robustness under domain shift. Designed for oncology tasks such as therapeutic target discovery and patient stratification where label sparsity and sample bias are prevalent.

DCAST: Diverse Class-Aware Self-Training

Fairness • Selection-bias mitigation • Semi-supervised learning

A self-training framework that explicitly encourages class diversity in the pseudo-labeling loop, mitigating selection bias and improving fairness across under-represented subgroups.

ELISL: Early–Late Integrated Synthetic Lethality

Multi-omics • Representation learning • Synthetic lethality

A hybrid early–late integration pipeline that leverages contextual omics features and learned embeddings to predict synthetic-lethality interactions with enhanced generalization.
